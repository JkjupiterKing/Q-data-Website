Material Master

The Material Master is a central database in ERP systems like SAP or Oracle, containing all essential information about materials used across procurement, inventory, production, sales, and distribution. It ensures all departments access consistent and accurate data, acting as the single source of truth.

Material Master in Business Operations

By centralizing material-related data, the Material Master improves decision-making, streamlines operations, and ensures better control of resources. Proper management leads to greater efficiency, improved customer satisfaction, and increased profitability.

Key Aspects of Material Master

  • Material Types: Raw Materials, Semi-Finished Goods, Finished Goods, Consumables, Services
  • Basic Data: Material Number, Description, Unit of Measure, Material Group
  • Inventory Management: Stock Levels, Storage Locations, Valuation Class, Batch Management
  • Procurement Data: Vendor Info, Purchasing Group, Purchasing Info Records
  • Sales & Distribution: Sales Org, Price Conditions, Shipping Conditions
  • Quality Management: Quality Control Specs, Inspection Settings
  • Work Scheduling: Bill of Materials (BOM), Production Version

Functions and Importance of Material Master

  • Centralization: A single source of truth for all departments
  • Inventory Efficiency: Manage stock levels and reorder points
  • Procurement & Supplier Management: Track pricing, delivery, vendor info
  • Manufacturing Optimization: Link with BOM and planning
  • Sales & Distribution: Control pricing and delivery terms
  • Financial Accuracy: Valuation class supports accounting and pricing
  • Regulatory Compliance: Ensure tracking and certifications

Material Master in ERP Systems

In ERP systems like SAP, Material Master data is split into departmental views:

  • Basic Data View: Common material details
  • Purchasing View: Supplier and pricing data
  • Sales View: Customer and shipping info
  • Accounting View: Valuation and cost control
  • Plant View: Location-specific stock and inventory settings

Best Practices in Material Master Management

  • Data Consistency: Regular audits and validation
  • System Integration: Seamless flow across procurement, production, and finance
  • User Access Control: Role-based data access
  • Data Maintenance: Regular updates for market and vendor changes
  • Standardization: Consistent naming and structuring
  • Automation: Auto-updates via supplier feeds and contracts
  • Quality Control: Manage certified and regulated materials effectively

Challenges in Material Master Management

  • Redundant data entries leading to confusion and inefficiency
  • Inaccurate or outdated material data
  • Complexity in managing vast and diverse material types
