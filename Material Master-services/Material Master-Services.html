Material Master Services



The Material Master is a crucial component of any business's ERP system and plays a vital role in ensuring efficient operations across procurement, inventory management, production, sales, and finance. By centralizing all material-related data, businesses can improve decision-making, streamline processes, and maintain better control over their resources. Proper management of the Material Master leads to more efficient business operations, better customer satisfaction, and ultimately, higher profitability.


Key Aspects of Material Master




  • Material Types:
    • Raw Materials: Basic materials used in the production process (e.g., steel, chemicals).
    • Semi-Finished Goods: Materials that have undergone partial processing but are not yet ready for sale (e.g., subassemblies, components).
    • Finished Goods: Completed products ready for sale or distribution.
    • Consumables: Materials used in daily operations that are consumed and not resold (e.g., office supplies, lubricants).
    • Services: Non-material goods that are required for business processes (e.g., maintenance services).
  • Basic Data:
    • Material Number: A unique identifier assigned to each material.
    • Description: A detailed name or description of the material.
    • Unit of Measure: The standard unit in which the material is measured (e.g., kilograms, liters, pieces).
    • Material Group: A classification for grouping materials of similar nature or usage.
  • Inventory Management:
    • Stock Levels: Information on the current inventory levels, minimum and maximum stock, and reorder points.
    • Storage Locations: The physical locations or warehouses where the material is stored.
    • Valuation Class: Defines how the material is valued in terms of accounting (e.g., raw materials, finished goods).
    • Batch Management: For materials that require tracking by batch or lot number for quality control or traceability.
  • Procurement Data:
    • Vendor Information: Suppliers from whom the material is procured, including price, delivery time, and conditions.
    • Purchasing Group: Defines the department or group responsible for procuring the material.
    • Purchasing Info Records: Specific conditions for purchasing a material, such as pricing agreements or discounts.
  • Sales and Distribution Data:
    • Sales Org: Sales organizations that handle the materialâ€™s distribution.
    • Price Conditions: Pricing details for the material, including base price, discounts, and other terms.
    • Shipping Conditions: The delivery requirements and instructions for the material.
  • Quality Management Data:
    • Quality Control: Information about the quality inspection procedures, tests, and standards that the material needs to meet.
    • Inspection Settings: Specifications for handling material inspections before accepting delivery.
  • Work Scheduling and Production Data:
    • Bill of Materials (BOM): Information on how the material is used in production processes (if it's a semi-finished or raw material).
    • Production Version: Defines different methods of manufacturing a material or product.

Functions and Importance of Material Master



  • Centralization of Material Data: The Material Master serves as a single source of truth for all material-related data, ensuring that information is consistent across departments. Whether it's procurement, inventory management, sales, or production, all teams work with the same data, reducing errors and miscommunication.
  • Efficient Inventory Management: The Material Master contains vital information regarding stock levels, reorder points, and storage locations. This helps in optimizing inventory by ensuring that materials are available when needed without excessive overstocking, thus reducing carrying costs.
  • Improved Procurement and Supplier Relationships: With detailed procurement data, businesses can easily manage vendor relationships, track pricing agreements, and ensure that procurement processes are streamlined. This helps in making informed decisions about where and how to source materials.
  • Production and Manufacturing Optimization: In manufacturing environments, the Material Master ensures that the right materials are available for production. By linking to production planning systems and Bills of Materials (BOM), it ensures that all necessary components are available when required, improving production efficiency.
  • Sales and Distribution Efficiency: For sales and distribution, the Material Master allows businesses to define the necessary parameters for material availability, pricing, and delivery conditions, enabling smoother order fulfillment and better customer satisfaction
  • Financial and Cost Control: The valuation class within the Material Master helps define how material costs are accounted for in financial reporting. Accurate material data helps in tracking expenses, calculating costs, and making strategic pricing decisions.
  • Regulatory Compliance: The Material Master can be configured to track regulatory compliance related to material sourcing, usage, and disposal. This is particularly important for industries that deal with controlled substances or require certifications, like pharmaceuticals or food manufacturing.

Material Master in ERP Systems


In ERP systems like SAP, the Material Master is integral to the efficient functioning of business processes. For example, in SAP, the Material Master is structured into different views that can be maintained for various departments or modules:


  • Basic Data View: Contains general information about the material such as name, number, and description.
  • Purchasing View:Defines data relevant to purchasing, such as the supplier and pricing conditions.
  • Sales View:Specifies sales data, including customer-specific pricing and shipping information.
  • Accounting View: Includes financial data, such as valuation class and price control.
  • Plant View: Includes material data specific to individual plants or locations, including stock levels and inventory settings.

Material Master Data Management Best Practices


Ensure that material master data is consistent and accurate across all departments. Regular audits and data validation can help maintain the integrity of the data.

Integrate the Material Master with other business systems such as procurement, production planning, and financial accounting to ensure seamless operations across the organization.

Restrict access to material master data based on roles and responsibilities. Sensitive material information should be protected from unauthorized access.

Regularly update and review material master data to account for changes in material usage, vendor information, pricing, and regulatory requirements. This ensures that businesses can respond quickly to changes in the market.

Implement standard naming conventions and consistent data structures to avoid duplication and confusion. Standardizing materials helps improve efficiency and data accuracy.

Use automated systems for updating and maintaining the Material Master, such as integrating supplier data feeds or automatically updating material prices when vendor contracts change.

Establish strong quality control mechanisms in the Material Master, particularly for materials that require certifications or need to pass quality checks.


Challenges in Material Master Management


  • Data Redundancy: If material master data is not properly maintained, duplication of records can occur, which can lead to inefficiencies, mistakes in procurement, and confusion in inventory management.
  • Data Accuracy: Incorrect or outdated material data (e.g., pricing, stock levels, lead times) can lead to stock outs, production delays, and misinformed decision-making.
  • Complexity: Managing large volumes of material data across various business functions can become complex, especially if there are many material types and categories.
  • Data Governance: Establishing clear data governance processes is essential to ensure the integrity and accuracy of the Material Master. This includes defining who is responsible for maintaining and updating material records.
  • Integration Issues: Integrating the Material Master with other enterprise systems, such as procurement, sales, and inventory management, can sometimes be challenging, especially if different systems use different data structures or formats.
